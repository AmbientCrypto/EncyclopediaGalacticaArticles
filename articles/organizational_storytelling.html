Despite its potent benefits, organizational storytelling is not a panacea and faces legitimate critiques. A primary concern is the risk of oversimplification and loss of nuance. The immense power of narrative necessitates careful navigation of complex ethical dilemmas. Foremost among these is the tension between Authenticity vs. Manipulation. The double-edged nature of organizational storytelling underscores that its power is neither inherently good nor bad, but contingent upon its purpose, authenticity, and ethical application. 9.2 Qualitative Assessment Methods: Capturing Depth and Meaning
