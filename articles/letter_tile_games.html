Tile Set Design: The Heart of the Game lies in the composition and physicality of the tiles themselves. Butts’ revolutionary frequency analysis, based on the New York Times, established the gold standard: common vowels and consonants like E (12 tiles), A, I, O (9 each), and R, T, N, S (6 each) appear abundantly, while high-scoring challenges like Q, Z, X, and J are limited to a single tile each. The inclusion and rules governing blank or wild tiles are equally critical. Serving as linguistic jokers, they offer crucial flexibility, allowing players to complete words or form high-scoring combinations. However, their power necessitates restrictions; in Scrabble, they carry no intrinsic point value and cannot be reused once played, preventing runaway advantages. The physical embodiment of the tiles also matters.
