As aircraft evolved from fragile wood-and-fabric biplanes to slightly more robust designs carrying passengers and mail, the inherent dangers became starkly apparent. The rapid technological advancements during World War I accelerated aircraft complexity, introducing more powerful engines, metal components, and intricate systems, further highlighting the inadequacy of relying solely on intuitive tinkering and underscoring the urgent need for standardized skills and accountability.

While SARPs are not direct law, ICAO member states (currently 193) are obligated under the Chicago Convention to notify ICAO of any differences between their national regulations and the SARPs.
